## What's here

- **`index.html`** — the codex: the Great Work in three books, the three operators (Virgil = Mercury, Beatrice = Sulphur, Bernard = the Quintessence), the apparatus of correspondences, and the numerology (100 cantos, the three colours, each book ending on *stelle*).
- **`inferno.html`** — Nigredo: the nine circles as strata of dissolution, the frozen lead at the center, and the hinge where *down becomes up* (solve → coagula).
- **`purgatorio.html`** — Albedo: the Mountain as the upright alembic, the seven terraces as the planetary ladder washed lead → silver, Lethe & Eunoe, the handover from Mercury to the Soul.
- **`paradiso.html`** — Rubedo: the nine spheres as the planetary distillation to gold (even leaden Saturn raised to a golden ladder), the Celestial Rose as the multiplied Stone, the squared circle, the conjunction.

## The tinfoil seal (honesty)

This is the **hermetic reading** — a centuries-old way of taking the *Commedia* as encoded alchemy (the colour-stages, the planetary ascent, the Fedeli d'Amore). It is offered as a **codex and a lens**, art over assertion: not a claim that Dante kept a furnace, but that the shape of his poem and the shape of the Work are the same shape. The **commentary here is original**; the *Commedia* (c. 1308–1320) is **public domain**, and quotation is kept to a famous line or two (the gate inscription via Longfellow's 1867 public-domain translation; the public-domain Italian closing lines). The gold is the reading, and the reading is ours.
