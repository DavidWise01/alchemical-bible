PARADISO

Rubedo · the reddening
operation · conjunction · multiplication · coagulaluminary · ☉ Sol / goldcolour · ◉ red

l'amor che move il sole e l'altre stelle— the last line, Paradiso XXXIII (Dante, original Italian, public domain) · “the Love that moves the sun and the other stars”

The white is not the end. Wash the matter to silver and you have purity, but purity is cold; it must be brought back to life, reddened, married. The rubedo is the last and highest stage — the reddening, the coniunctio, the wedding of the white queen and the red king, the moment the silver takes fire and becomes gold. This is the Stone. Dante, washed white at the summit of the mountain, now rises — and the rising itself is the distillation, sphere after sphere, planet after planet, until what climbs is no longer lead, nor silver, but gold.

The nine spheres — the planetary ladder, distilled to gold

each heaven a sublimation; the Sun at the heart

Where Purgatory climbed the planets down in weight to reach the white, Paradise climbs them up in glory to reach the red. Beatrice — the Soul, the fixing Sulphur — draws Dante through nine concentric heavens, each a finer distillation of the spirit, each redeeming the very flaw the matching terrace had purged. The lead of pride becomes the gold of wisdom; the same metal, transmuted.

Watch the seventh sphere: Saturn, lead, the very metal frozen at the bottom of Hell, now appears as a golden ladder climbing out of sight. The element that was the dead nadir of the nigredo is, by the rubedo, raised into light. That is the whole doctrine in one image: nothing is discarded; everything is transmuted. The lead is not replaced by gold. The lead becomes gold.

The Empyrean — the Rose, the Stone

beyond the spheres: pure light, and the perfected matter unfolding

Past the ninth sphere there is no more where and no more when — only the Empyrean, pure light. A river of fire becomes, as Dante's eyes adjust, an immense white-and-red Rose: the Candida Rosa, every petal a soul, the perfected matter blooming open at the end of the Work. This is the multiplication — the final alchemical operation, where the Stone, once made, increases without limit, turning all it touches to its own gold. The Rose is the Stone made social: one perfection multiplied into a city of light.

The squared circle — the conjunction completes

the impossible figure; the Stone achieved

At the very last, Dante is given the vision: three circles, one Trinity, interpenetrating — and within them, the human image. He strains to understand how the human face fits the circle, "like the geometer who sets himself to square the circle and cannot find the measure." The squaring of the circle: the alchemists' oldest emblem for the Work complete — the union of the four (the square, matter) with the one (the circle, spirit), the marriage of the human and the divine. He cannot do it by force; in a flash, his will and desire are turned like a wheel, evenly, by the Love that moves the sun and the other stars.

That is the coniunctio. The Stone. The third and final surfacing into the stelle — and this time the Work does not merely lift its eyes to the stars; it is moved by what moves them. Lead has become gold. The descent that began in a dark wood ends in the gold at the center of the light.

the operation, summed. Conjunction · multiplication · coagula. Sol / gold. Red. The whitened matter is raised through nine planetary distillations — even the leaden Saturn transmuted to a golden ladder — to the Rose and the squared circle: the soul wed to the divine, the Stone made and multiplied. The Great Work is finished. One hundred cantos. Three colours. One gold.
